How We Work

These 6 plays form the foundation of how we aim to deliver more successful projects. They are drawn from established successful practices of both the private and public sector.

  1. Understand what people needEvery project must start by exploring the needs and problems of the people who will use the application (note that this is probably not the funders!)

  2. Make it simple and intuitive Better to have multiple apps that do different things brilliantly, than one app that does everything badly

  3. Build using agile and iterative practicesGet the product in the hands of users as early as possible, so we can learn fast and have the best chance of success

  4. Choose a modern technology stack and default to openWe need to work efficiently and openely, building maintainable and scaleable applications, avoiding vendor lock-in, and enabling other teams to extend and iterate on our work

  5. Make decisions based on data, not suppositionAt every stage of the project we should establish processes and metrics which enable us to use data to guide and evidence development and design decision making

  6. Involve all stakeholders throughout the projectProjects are successful when everyone in the team engages and plays an active role in decision making. In this case, the 'team' means everyone with a stake in the outcome of the project, from the developers to the project sponsor and the ultimate users of the application

Skipping any one of these plays puts the successful delivery of the project at significant risk.
